# Phone Pitch Script: Andrew Bluestone

**Goal of the Call:** Do NOT sell the software on the first call. The goal is entirely to secure a 5-minute Zoom/screen-share meeting or get permission to email him the live Render link so he can play with it himself.

---

### Phase 1: The Hook (First 15 Seconds)
*(Be direct, professional, and skip the small talk. Lawyers bill by the minute.)*

"Hi Andrew, my name is [Your Name]. I know you’re incredibly busy, so I'll be brief. 

I’ve been reading the New York Attorney Malpractice Blog, and the sheer volume of case law you’ve published over the years is incredible. 

I'm calling because I actually built a custom piece of AI software trained exclusively on your brain—specifically, all 4,000 of your articles—and I want to give it to you to test out."

---

### Phase 2: The Value Prop (If he asks "What does it do?")
*(Keep it focused on his pain points: wasted time and teaching).*

"It essentially turns your written archive into a 24/7 virtual associate. 

I've built it with two modes:

**First**, a 'Client Mode' that sits on your website. When a prospect visits, it instantly analyzes their situation against your case law. It acts as an intake filter to automatically screen out bad leads—like blown statutes of limitations—before they ever reach your desk.

**Second**, I built a 'Professor Mode' for your students at St. John's. Instead of answering questions, it acts like you. It uses the Socratic method to critique your law students' reasoning and generates hypothetical malpractice scenarios based on your exact cases."

---

### Phase 3: The Call to Action (The Ask)
"I’m not calling to sell you anything today. I genuinely just want to show you what your life's work looks like when it's interactive. 

I have a live link ready. Can I email it over to you right now so you can ask it a complex malpractice question on your phone? 

Or, if you have 5 minutes next Tuesday, I’d love to pull it up on a screen share and show you exactly how it instantly cites your own precedent."

---

### Handling Objections

**Objection: "I don't trust AI. It hallucinates."**
"I completely agree. That’s why this is what’s called a 'Retrieval-Augmented' system. It is securely walled off from the open internet. It physically cannot generate an answer unless it pulls the exact citation and URL from one of your published blog posts first."

**Objection: "Send me some information." (The brush-off)**
"Absolutely. What is the best email to send the live prototype link to? Once you click it, try putting it in 'Professor Mode' and argue a weak continuous representation claim. You'll love how aggressively it critiques you using your own articles."
